Congee

Description

Also known as jook, this traditional savory Chinese breakfast porridge is quick and easy, made using pork, rice, oyster sauce and ginger. It is topped with sliced spring onions, salted duck egg and pepper. Great flavor and flexible.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup long grain white rice
  • 1 teaspoon vegetable oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 pound boneless, pork loin roast
  • 5 cups water
  • 1.5 teaspoons oyster sauce
  • 1 salted (hard-cooked) duck egg, chopped
  • 1 hundred-year egg, minced
  • 1 piece fresh ginger root, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup chopped green onion
  • ground black pepper to taste
  • 3 spring onions, sliced
  • soy sauce to taste

Steps

  1. Rinse and drain rice, and place in a large pot. Stir in salt and oil, and let stand for 5 minutes
  2. Add pork to the rice, stir in the water. Bring to a boil, then simmer over medium heat for 15 mnutes, or until the pork is cooked through.
  3. Remove the pork from the pot with a slotted spoon, and set aside
  4. Continue to simmer the rice for 20 minutes.
  5. Chop the pork into small cubes, and mix with the salted egg and hundred-year egg
  6. After the 20 minutes are up, stir the pork and egg mixture back into the congee along with the oyster sauce.
  7. Garnish with ginger and green onion. Have soy sauce and pepper on the side for seasoning.
